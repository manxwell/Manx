Part 1. Ethics in Ancient Philosophy

Plato, Euthyphro

Video Lecture:

Socrates's Question:

What is piety, and what is impiety?

i.e. What is virtue?

Euthyphro's Answer:

Piety, then is that which is dear to the gods, and impiety is that which is not dear to them.

i.e. Virtue is what the gods love.

Socrates's Question (explanatory priority):

The point which I should first wish to understand is whether the pious or holy is beloved by the gods because it is holy, or holy because it is beloved of the gods.

i.e. Are virtuous acts virtuous because the gods love them, or do the gods love them because they are virtuous?

Euthyphro's Answer:

Gods love them because they are virtuous.

Socrates:

$\dots$ you apear to me, Euthyphro, when I ask you what is essence of holyness, to offer an attribute only, and not the essence $\dots$ tell me once more what holiness or piety really is, whether dear to the gods or not $\dots$ and what is impiety?

i.e. Your answer doesn't explain anything.

Euthyphro:

Great, I'm confused.
