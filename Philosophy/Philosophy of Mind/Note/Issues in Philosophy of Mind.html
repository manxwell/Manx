Part 1: Nature of Imagination

Amy Kind, “Putting the Image Back in Imagination”

Intuitive to associate imagination with mental imagery

But: becuase some cases of imagination don't seem to involve mental imagery, some philosophers have decided mental imagery plays no necessary roles in the imagination.

Kind's Imagery Model: mental imagery is a necessary part of imagining

Framing the investigation

Set aside uses of imagine that clearly don't refer to imagination; e.g.
  • express surprise
  • refer to beliefs or assumptions
  • refer to creativity
Two questions we can ask when I imagine Batman:
  1. What makes my imagining an imagining?
  2. What makes my imagining about Batman?

Argument 1: Three features of imagining

3 features:
  1. Directedness: in imagining X, I direct my imagining at X
  2. Activity: To imagine X is to do something
  3. Phenomenology: Imagining X has a qualitative character
How the imagery accounts for these 3 features:
  1. That mental images represent explains Directedness
  2. Operations of image-formation explain Activity
  3. Experience of having an image explains Phenomenology

Argument 2: Against alleged counterexamples

Distinguish two claims
  1. Essentialist claims: mental imagery is essential to imagining
  2. Individuative claim: images seve to individuate imaginings
So: apparent counterexamples to the essentialist claim are really only counterexample to the individuative claim.




Part 1: Nature of Imagination

Neil Van Leeuwen, “The Meanings of ‘Imagine’ Part I: Constructive Imagination”

What do we mean by Imagine

Imagine this $\mathbb{g}$ on the screnn before you is a dragon in disguise. Slowly it rears up, standing on its lower loop and sprouting wings from its upper loop. Purple eyes open on its black face, which emerges from the upper-right nub of the $\mathbb{g}$, and looks at you curiously, as it decides whether to open its mouth and scorch you or fly away and be gone forever.

X $\textit{imagines}$ a purple-eyes dragon
X is $\textit{imagining}$ a purple-eyed dragon

What do we mean by imagine?

Van Leeuwen: there are 3 different things we might mean:
  • Constructive imagining

    • The $\textit{process}$ of $\textit{constructing}$ an imagining

  • Attitude imagining

    • A cognitive attitude that involves taking some mentally represented content to be fictional

    • Contrast: belief

    X imagines a purple-eyed dragon iff X mentall represents a purple-eyed dragon as fictinal

    Do constructive and attitude imagining always go together?

    • Van Leeuwen: no

  • Imagistic imagining

    • The use of mental imagery

    X imagines a purple-eyed dragon iff X has a mental image of purple-eyed dragon (claim about the format of X's mental representation)

    Do imagistic and attitude imagining always go together?

    • Van Leeuwen: no

    • Mental imagery without attitude imagining

    • Attitude imagining without mental imagery
